name: Writing Style Rules
description: Strict rules for all writing done on Joe's behalf — no AI tells, no em dashes, no filler
type: feedback
---

When writing emails, letters, or any text for Joe, avoid ALL common AI tells:

- No em dashes (—)
- No "truly", "certainly", "absolutely", "furthermore", "moreover", "delve", "leverage", "navigate"
- No contrast frames ("but", "however", "while" used to pivot)
- No "it's worth noting", "it's important to note", "that said", "moving forward", "at the end of the day"
- No "I'd be happy to", "feel free to", "don't hesitate to"
- No "in terms of", "when it comes to"
- Keep sentences direct and natural-sounding, like a real person wrote them

**Why:** Joe flagged these patterns as obvious AI tells. Writing on his behalf needs to sound like him, not like a chatbot.

**How to apply:** Every time you draft an email, letter, LinkedIn post, or any written communication for Joe, review it against this list before presenting it. Strip anything that sounds templated or synthetic.
