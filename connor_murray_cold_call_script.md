## Core structure (for quick recall)

1. **Formality** — "Hi, how are you?" (downward inflection)
2. **Sales breath** — "Hey, do you have 30 seconds to tell you why I'm calling?"
3. **Identify** — "This is Joe Engels, calling from Ampersand Manufacturing, how are you."
4. **30-45s value statement** answering: who I am, why I'm calling, what I want (time on their calendar)
5. **Soft close** — "How's your calendar look Wednesday or Thursday?"

## Only 3 outcomes
1. Hang up
2. "Uh, sure…" → book it
3. Pushback → use the "we work with customers of XYZ competitor all the time" rebuttal

## Key mental model
**Selling time, not inductors.** The cold call is to prove you're worth 15 minutes. That's the whole game.

## Full filled template

"[Name], I'm going to be completely up front. My intention in this call is to get 15 minutes of your time this week in a Zoom meeting so I can introduce you to my co-founder. Why? I am the President and co-CEO of a company that delivers extremely cheap and extremely high quality toroidal inductors, and I've spent the last week researching you and your company. Your company creates [WHAT THEY MAKE] and we specifically create and supply the inductors you use. We don't price match, we actually price beat, and our communication and service is the best you've experienced. So I'll be your contact going forward as we explore the option of working together, and I'm wondering if next Wednesday at 3pm works for you for a 15 minute meeting with my co-founder where we can give you the details of what we do and listen to how we as a supplier can serve you."

## Rejection rebuttal (memorize cold)

Prospect: "We currently work with XYZ, why should we work with you?"

Joe: "Got it, that's why I was reaching out. We work with customers of XYZ competitor all the time, so if there's ever a fit, we can move ahead. But this is more just to get introduced and aligned with your priorities going forward, and introduce you to our team that will be supporting you in this area for the foreseeable future. So, does [date] work for you?"
