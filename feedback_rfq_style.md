Joe's RFQ style is direct, lean, and sets the terms. No warm intros, no "my name is," no explaining what Ampersand does. The supplier should feel like they're being evaluated, not courted.

**Key patterns:**
- Opens with what he's sourcing, one sentence
- Specs in a clean bulleted list
- States price as a fact, not a "target" -- no hedging
- "Zero-defect standard" / "non-negotiable" language on quality
- Gives a deadline for response ("respond by end of day Wednesday")
- Closes with leverage: "We're building a supplier relationship with volume potential. This order proves whether you can be that partner."
- Sign-off: Joe Engels, Co-Founder, Co-CEO, Ampersand Manufacturing

**What NOT to do:**
- No "Dear Sales Team" -- use their name if known
- No "My name is Joe Engels and I'm the owner of..." -- skip it
- No explaining what Ampersand does or where it's based
- No "looking for a reliable manufacturing partner" -- too soft
- No "I'm happy to send a drawing" -- attach it or say "spec attached"
- No asking "can you provide samples?" -- tell them what you need
- Don't say "target price" -- state the price

**Template RFQ (Minerva-12 to Michael as model):**

My company, Ampersand Manufacturing, is interested in potentially switching suppliers for a part we use.

Full spec attached: [drawing number].

Order Details:
- Quantity: [number]
- Material: [material]
- Specification: [core name], exact dimensions per [drawing]
- Quality: 100% QC inspection required. Zero-defect standard.
- Timeline: ASAP
- Price: $X.XX/pc
- Coating: [type or None]
- Shipping: [FOB / EXW]
- Payment: Wire transfer upon sample approval

[Specific ask about capability]

Can you execute at this price and timeline?

If yes, respond by [deadline] with:
1. Your lead time
2. When you can begin sample prep after payment

We're building a supplier relationship with volume potential. This order proves whether you can be that partner.

Joe Engels
Co-Founder, Co-CEO, Ampersand Manufacturing

**CRITICAL -- Minerva-3 epoxy is MOLDED ENCAPSULATION, not dip coat:**
- The Minerva-3 spec calls for 0.015" (0.381mm) epoxy. That is WAY too thick for dip coating (which gives 0.002-0.005").
- This is a MOLDED epoxy encapsulation requiring a custom mold for the core dimensions.
- Mold fees apply and can be significant. Always ask suppliers to quote mold fee separately.
- Do NOT call it "coating" -- call it "molded epoxy encapsulation" in RFQs.
- Ampersand has been charged mold fees for this core before. This is a known cost.
- I (Claude) got this wrong initially by assuming wound toroidal cores have no mold fees. The WINDING has no mold, but the ENCAPSULATION does. Don't make this mistake again.

**CRITICAL -- Drawing conventions:**
- "Rev_R" in a filename means it's the REDACTED version, safe to send to suppliers
- Original NK drawings (without Rev_R) are internal only -- NEVER send to suppliers

**CRITICAL -- Naming convention:**
- NEVER use "NK" anything with suppliers. All NK Technologies part names are redacted and replaced with "Minerva" branding.
- NK-3 = Minerva-3, NK-4 = Minerva-4, NK-12 = Minerva-12, NK-20 = Minerva-20, NK-70 = Minerva-70
- Drawings must be redacted to remove "NK" references, NK part numbers, and NK Technologies branding before sending to any supplier
- This protects sourcing information and prevents suppliers from identifying the end customer

**Why:** Joe's style signals competence and leverage. He's not begging for quotes -- he's telling them the terms and seeing who can perform. This filters out time-wasters fast.
**How to apply:** Use this template for ALL supplier outreach. Never soften the tone. Never add filler. Always use Minerva naming, never NK.
